Methodology & Standards

How this document is built, and what it will not do.

  • The target is the claim, not the person. The same claim is pushed by dozens of accounts; the counter-evidence exists independently of who says it. This page is structured around the claim as the primary unit; accounts are the amplifier map.
  • Direct attribution only for public figures and registered organisations. Elected officials, registered political entities, media outlets, and accounts that have branded themselves as public political actors. Their statements are public acts.
  • Pseudonymous accounts stay pseudonymous. Handles are catalogued as they appear; no attempt is made to unmask. Their role in the ecosystem is recorded at the tier level, not the individual-identity level.
  • Counter-evidence requires sources rated ★★☆ or higher. Government data, peer-reviewed research, and court rulings are the backbone. Advocacy documents and anecdotes are marked as such on both sides.
  • This is a living document. Individual-account specific-claim attribution requires dated screenshot / URL evidence collected through the project's cross-platform research pipeline. Slots marked "attribution pending" are intentional—they get filled as evidence arrives, not before.
  • Legitimate grievances are acknowledged. On every talking point, the "what's accurate" block goes first. Credibility depends on conceding what is real before examining what is exaggerated or false.

About this directory

Tiers 1–3 are registered organisations and public media outlets. Their positions are documented from official statements, platform content, and public records.
